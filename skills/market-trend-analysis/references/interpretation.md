# Interpretation Guide — market-trend-analysis

## Reading the Composite Verdict

The verdict synthesizes four components with different weights. Here's how to interpret the combinations:

### Strong Bullish (score > 5, HIGH conviction)

All components aligned bullish. The trend is clear, momentum supports it, volume confirms.
- **Action**: Buy / add to position. Best entry point for trend-following strategies.

### Moderate Bullish (score 2-5, MEDIUM conviction)

Most components bullish but one may be neutral or slightly bearish.
- **Action**: Buy with tighter risk management. The weaker component may be a warning.

### Cautious Bullish (score 0.5-2, LOW conviction)

Slight bullish lean but significant disagreement among components.
- **Action**: Wait or scale in small. Check why components conflict — is RSI oversold in a bearish EMA structure? That's a bounce, not a trend.

### Neutral (score -0.5 to 0.5)

No clear direction. Components cancel each other out.
- **Action**: Stay flat. No edge.

### Cautious Bearish (score -2 to -0.5)

Slight bearish lean with disagreement.
- **Action**: Reduce or hedge. Look for confirmation before shorting.

### Moderate Bearish (score -5 to -2)

Most components bearish.
- **Action**: Sell / stay out. The trend is against you.

### Strong Bearish (score < -5)

All components bearish. Full alignment downward.
- **Action**: Exit longs, consider shorts if risk allows.

## Common Conflict Patterns

### "EMA bearish + RSI oversold"
The long-term trend is down but momentum is washed out. This is a **bounce candidate**, not a trend reversal. The bounce may be tradeable but the trend will likely resume down.

### "EMA bullish + RSI overbought"
Strong uptrend with stretched momentum. The trend is intact but a pullback or consolidation is likely. Don't buy here — wait for the RSI to cool.

### "Squeeze bullish + Volume diverging"
Price coiling for a breakout but volume is not confirming. The breakout may be a false signal. Wait for volume to join.

### "EMA bullish + Squeeze bearish fading"
The long-term picture is positive but short-term momentum is running out. A pullback in the uptrend. This can be a good entry point once the squeeze turns bullish again.
